Electronics and Communication Engineering

M.Tech. in Electronics and Communications is a two-year postgraduate degree program for engineering students. Electronics and communication is an allied branch of engineering which deals with research, design, manufacturing, and testing of electronic and communication devices. The program equips candidates with the skill sets and specialization required in present-day digital and electronic communication. The program provides in-depth knowledge and practical exposure to candidates in areas such as Electronics and Computers, System Integration, Design, Testing, Digital and Wireless Network Technologies, Digital and Embedded Systems, Software Systems, etc. The M. Tech. The program also focuses on developing business acumen in engineers to provide them maximum career options once they complete the degree program.

Computer Science and Engineering

M.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering is one of the specialized degrees in the Masters Programme for Computer Science enthusiasts. It is an ideal course for students who are more inclined towards the technological aspect in their Bachelor in Computer Science Degree. The course makes the students to have a good understanding of the theoretical foundations and limits of computing, adapting to existing models, techniques, algorithms and data structures. The programme develops an ability to undertake original research in cutting edge computer science and related areas. Elective papers include Modern Algorithm Design, Randomised Algorithm, Graduate Algorithm with Computer Architecture and Programme Analysis.

Mechanical Engineering (Thermal Engineering)

In the M.Tech. Program, the emphasis is on the development of a broad background in a particular stream followed by a deeper study of a problem in the stream. The course covers real-time fluid flow and heat transfer applications in Thermal Energy Systems, Cryogenic Engineering, Refrigeration & Air Conditioning, and other fields.

Electrical & Electronics Engineering (Power Electronics)

Power electronics is the area of engineering that focuses on applying solid-state electronics to the transformation and management of electrical power. In basic terms, it is the study of changing electrical power to different forms. This focus of the field typically relates to the conversion and control of electrical power, but can also pertain to research related to the design, computation, management and assimilation of the systems. The goal is to control the energy’s flow from source to load in the most efficient, reliable, cost-effective way possible.
